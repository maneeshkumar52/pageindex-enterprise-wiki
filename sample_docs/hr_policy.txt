# HR Policy Manual — Acme Corporation

## 1. Introduction

This document outlines the Human Resources policies and procedures for all employees of Acme Corporation. It is the responsibility of each employee to read, understand, and comply with these policies.

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025
**Last Revised:** March 15, 2025
**Applies to:** All full-time, part-time, and contract employees

## 2. Employment Categories

### 2.1 Full-Time Employees
Employees working 40 hours per week on a regular basis. Full-time employees are eligible for the complete benefits package including health insurance, retirement contributions, and paid time off.

### 2.2 Part-Time Employees
Employees working fewer than 30 hours per week. Part-time employees receive prorated benefits based on hours worked.

### 2.3 Contract Employees
Individuals engaged for a specific project or duration. Contract employees are not eligible for company benefits unless explicitly stated in their agreement.

## 3. Leave Policies

### 3.1 Annual Leave
- **New employees (0-1 year):** 15 days per year, accrued monthly.
- **Employees (1-5 years):** 20 days per year.
- **Senior employees (5+ years):** 25 days per year.
- Leave must be requested at least 2 weeks in advance for periods exceeding 3 days.
- Unused leave can be carried forward up to a maximum of 10 days.

### 3.2 Sick Leave
- All employees receive 12 sick days per calendar year.
- A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.
- Sick leave cannot be carried forward or encashed.

### 3.3 Parental Leave
- **Primary caregiver:** 16 weeks of paid leave.
- **Secondary caregiver:** 4 weeks of paid leave.
- Parental leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or adoption.

### 3.4 Bereavement Leave
- 5 days of paid leave for the loss of an immediate family member.
- 3 days for extended family members.

## 4. Code of Conduct

### 4.1 Professional Behaviour
All employees are expected to maintain professional conduct in the workplace. This includes:
- Treating colleagues with respect and dignity.
- Avoiding discrimination based on race, gender, religion, age, or disability.
- Maintaining confidentiality of proprietary information.
- Adhering to data protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA as applicable).

### 4.2 Conflict of Interest
Employees must disclose any potential conflicts of interest to their manager and the HR department. Outside employment or business activities that may conflict with company interests require prior written approval.

### 4.3 Anti-Harassment Policy
Acme Corporation maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment of any kind. Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it immediately to HR or use the anonymous reporting hotline.

## 5. Compensation and Benefits

### 5.1 Salary Reviews
- Annual performance reviews are conducted in December.
- Salary adjustments take effect on January 1 of the following year.
- Merit-based increases range from 3% to 8% based on performance rating.

### 5.2 Health Insurance
- Company-sponsored health insurance covers medical, dental, and vision.
- Coverage extends to spouse and dependents up to age 26.
- Premium cost-sharing: Company 80%, Employee 20%.

### 5.3 Retirement Plan
- 401(k) plan with company matching up to 6% of base salary.
- Employees are eligible after 90 days of employment.
- Vesting schedule: 25% per year over 4 years.

## 6. Remote Work Policy

### 6.1 Eligibility
Remote work arrangements are available to employees whose roles can be performed outside the office, subject to manager approval.

### 6.2 Guidelines
- Employees must maintain a dedicated workspace with reliable internet.
- Core working hours (10 AM - 3 PM local time) must be observed.
- Remote workers must be available on company communication channels.
- Equipment provided: laptop, monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset.

### 6.3 Hybrid Model
The company supports a hybrid model: minimum 2 days in-office per week. In-office days may be designated by department heads for team collaboration.

## 7. Grievance Procedure

1. **Informal Resolution:** Discuss the matter directly with the person involved.
2. **Manager Escalation:** If unresolved, raise the issue with your immediate supervisor.
3. **HR Mediation:** HR will facilitate a mediation session within 5 business days.
4. **Formal Complaint:** Submit a written complaint to the Grievance Committee.
5. **Committee Decision:** The committee will provide a binding decision within 15 business days.

## 8. Termination

### 8.1 Notice Period
- Employees with less than 1 year of service: 2 weeks notice.
- Employees with 1-5 years of service: 4 weeks notice.
- Senior employees and management: 8 weeks notice.

### 8.2 Exit Process
- Return all company property (laptop, badge, keys).
- Complete knowledge transfer documentation.
- Exit interview with HR within the final week.
- Final paycheck includes accrued leave and any applicable bonuses.
